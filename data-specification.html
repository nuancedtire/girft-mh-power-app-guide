A

Collection protocol

What to collect, when, and who does it.

INCLUDE

Exactly 10 consecutive cases

During the June 2026 collection period, collect one response for each of the first 10 consecutive patients whose primary reason for ED attendance is mental-health-related and whose ED stay exceeds 12 hours.

EXCLUDE

No patient identifiers

Do not enter name, NHS number, date of birth, address, post code, record number, or identifying narrative. The NHS survey is designed not to collect patient-identifiable data.

  1. Agree the acute hospital site, acute Trust, mental health Trust, named data collator and reviewer before the first case.
  2. Identify cases prospectively where feasible as they approach the 12-hour threshold. Retrospective collection is permitted, but timestamps may be unavailable.
  3. Start a case record for every eligible patient in sequence. Do not select “better” examples or skip a difficult case.
  4. Use the acute record for the early pathway and liaise with the MH Trust for MHLT, MHA, bed and transport information.
  5. Select Not recorded where the tool allows it. Do not invent a time or use an estimate.
  6. Reviewer quality-checks the record, then the nominated administrator manually submits one online NHS form per patient.
Interpretation: “MHLT response” means the first contact following referral, not the full assessment. “Assessment start” means the beginning of the full mental-health assessment. “Assessment complete” means the clinical decision was reached.
